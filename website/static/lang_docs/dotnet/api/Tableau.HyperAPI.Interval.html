Remarks

An interval consists of three logically independent components: number of months, number of days, and number of seconds (with microsecond precision). It can encode relative time offsets like "1 year" (equivalent to "12 months", but takes variable number of days depending on whether the year is a leap year), "1 month" (variable number of days depending on the month and whether the year is a leap year), "2 days" (which may or may not be equivalent to 48 hours depending on when daylight savings change happens), "3 hours 20 minutes".

These components are independent, and there is no automatic conversion. For example an interval of one day is not equal to an interval of 24*60*60 seconds.

Do not convert Interval to TimeSpan and back, except when the interval consists only of the microseconds component.

Arithmetic operations are performed independently on the month, day, and second parts.
